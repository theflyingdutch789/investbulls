ROBINHOOD INVESTORS GET REALITY CHECK AS NIFTY TUMBLED FOR 2ND STRAIGHT DAY THIS WEEK

Asian equities struggled to emulate Wall Street's rebound yesterday as persistent worries abt the global economic recovery kept investors cautious, while ebbing inflation expectations helped the U.S. $ to a 2-M high. Wall Street's rebound was led by a 1.7% gain in the Nasdaq as a broker upgrade lifted Amazon shares by nearly 6%. However, overall global mkts have been kept under chk with China-U.S. tensions simmering, Britain re-imposing some curbs on restaurants to try & head off a 2nd wave of coronavirus infections while the U.S. election campaign seems to be distracting Congress fm passing further aid bills. In commodities, oil prices slipped after a surprise rise in U.S. crude inventories. Brent trades arnd $41/bl currently. India’s equities fell for a 4th straight day, with investors continued piling into IT & Pharma stocks that are considered relatively insulated fm the country’s gloomy economic outlook. The Nifty50 index closed dn 97 pts, or 0.86%. Maruti & LT (both dn arnd 3%) were among the top Nifty losers. On the other hand, IT heavyweights - HCL Tech, Tech Mahindra & TCS gained over 2%. Amongst the broader mkt, GMM slipped 10% aftr fixing OFS price at a significant discount. Route Mobile gained 7% as Goldman Sachs, Kuwait Investment Authority picked stake. India & China ystrdy said they had agreed to strengthen communication, refrain fm sending more troops to the border & avoid actions that wud worsen tensions b/w the 2 countries. Separately, Lok Sabha ystrdy passed 3 imp labour codes that shall cheer industries & allow them flexibility in hiring & retrenchment, make industrial strikes difficult, facilitate ease of doing business & help expand social security net for informal workers. The Lok Sabha is likely to be adjourned sine die tdy, 8 days ahead of the scheduled end of the Monsoon Session, amid concerns of spread of coronavirus among lawmakers. Seperately, Rating agency ICRA suggested India's current a/c will swing to a surplus of $30 bn or 1.2% of GDP in FY21, due to slowdown in imports during the pandemic. Going ahead, mkts likely to be driven by global cues, in addition to expiry effect. SGX Nifty indicates flattish opening for Nifty today.
